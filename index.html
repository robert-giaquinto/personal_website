Welcome!

I'm a PhD student in the Computer Science department at the University of Minnesota. My advisor is Professor Arindam Banerjee.

Research

My research interests lie primarily in the intersection of machine learning and natural language processing. In particular, I'm interested in probabilistic graphical models, approximate inference, deep generative models, and information retrieval.

Deep Generative & Graphical Models

Probabilistic graphical models (PGM) describe observed data and hidden variables with probability distributions and capture their relationships. I've discovered novel PGMs for topic modeling on text data written by multiple authors over time (e.g. blogs or journals), and for recommendation systems that incorporate text data (e.g. ratings of movies/restaurants with written reviews).

Approximate Inference

Scaling probabilistic graphical models to large datasets is computationally intense. In my research I've developed inference algorithms for scaling complex PGMs, with difficult non-conjugate terms, to massive billion-word corpora. I'm also interested in algorithms for training deep generative models, like variational autoencoders.

Information Retrieval

My research has lead to new methods that can discover common "health journeys" experienced by patients and caregivers during serious health crises. I also have research experience in information retrieval for legal search and on electronic health records.
