About me.

I'm a master's student in the Computer Science department, and a research assistant with Natural Language Processing team in the Institute for Health Informatics at the University of Minnesota. Prior to graduate school I had the pleasure of working under Dr. Alex Ushveridze on Capella Education Company's predictive modeling team. I attended St. Olaf College where I studied mathematics and statistics, conducted research on alumni giving, and met my future wife Lindsey.

Research interests.

While my master's coursework has included a breadth of topics, my research interests lie primarily in the intersection of Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing. In particular I'm interested in discovering more flexible and reliable means of interacting with machines using natural language. In my lifetime, I would hope to contribute to a machine's ability able to grasp meaning from natural language at an intuitive level.
